How to Spend One Week in Salento — The Perfect Itinerary

Plan your week in Salento with our detailed itinerary. Explore stunning beaches, historic Lecce, and the charm of Puglian towns, using Mare Mediterraneo as your perfect starting point.

Photo by Massimo Virgilio on Unsplash

Why choose Salento for a week-long vacation?

Salento, a picturesque region at the heel of Italy's boot, offers a unique blend of stunning coastlines, rich history, and delicious cuisine. Its strategic location provides seamless access to a variety of attractions from sandy beaches to historic towns, making it the ideal destination for a week-long getaway. Staying at Mare Mediterraneo in Torre Chianca puts you in a perfect position to enjoy all that Salento has to offer, being just 400 meters from the sea and mere minutes from Lecce.

What can I do in and around Lecce?

Start your journey in Lecce, aptly dubbed the "Florence of the South." Just a 15-minute drive from Torre Chianca, Lecce is famed for its baroque architecture. Spend your day wandering through its narrow streets, visiting iconic spots like the Basilica di Santa Croce and Piazza del Duomo. Enjoy a leisurely lunch at one of the local trattorias to savor authentic Puglian cuisine like orecchiette pasta with turnip tops.

Which beaches are a must-visit?

Salento boasts beautiful beaches with crystal waters. Punta Prosciutto, located approximately 25 minutes from Mare Mediterraneo, is perfect for a day under the sun, known for its Caribbean-like waters. Alternatively, head to Porto Cesareo, around 30 minutes away, for sandy stretches and vibrant marine life. Both locations offer equipment rentals for an enhanced beach experience.

What historical sites can I explore?

For a touch of history, drive 35 minutes to Otranto, a charming coastal town with a historic castle and stunning sea views. Don’t miss the Otranto Cathedral, adorned with intricate mosaics. Continue your exploration further south in Gallipoli, about 45 minutes from Torre Chianca, where the old town sits on a limestone island connected by a bridge. Discover its maze of whitewashed streets and the beautiful Gallipoli Castle.

Where are the best places to eat in Salento?

Salento's culinary scene is a delightful journey in itself. In Lecce, try Le Zie Trattoria for a hearty, home-cooked meal in a setting that feels like visiting a local's home. In Gallipoli, savor fresh seafood at Trattoria del Mare, where the sea-to-table concept brings the tastes of the coast directly to your plate.

What day trips are recommended?

Consider a day trip to the stunning natural reserve of Torre Guaceto or explore the rugged cliffs and caves of Santa Maria di Leuca at the tip of the peninsula. Both locations offer an immersive experience into Salento's unspoiled nature.
