Tracing the Rich History of Salento: From Greeks to Baroque Splendour

Discover Salento's intricate history through its Greek, Roman, Norman, and Baroque influences, and learn why it remains a captivating destination for visitors.

Photo by Davide Perrone on Unsplash

Introduction to Salento's Historic Tapestry

Salento, the sun-drenched heel of Italy's boot-shaped peninsula, is not just a feast for the eyes with its beautiful beaches and inviting waters. It's also a rich historical tapestry that tells stories of ancient civilizations and contrasting empires. Situated conveniently for a journey through time, Torre Chianca is home to Mare Mediterraneo, your perfect launchpad for exploring this captivating region, with its luxurious Suite Pietra and Suite Cielo starting from 200 EUR per night.

Magna Graecia: The Greek Influence

Long before Salento gained fame as a tourist haven, it was part of the ancient Greek world known as Magna Graecia. Evidence of Greek settlements can still be found in places like Gallipoli and Otranto, merely 45 and 35 minutes from Torre Chianca respectively. These towns retain Greek architectural elements, especially Otranto with its impressive mosaic artwork in the cathedral. Don't miss the chance to explore these gems while staying at Mare Mediterraneo.

Roman Entrenchments

After the Greeks, the Romans arrived and left an indelible mark on Salento. Lecce, just a 15-minute drive from Torre Chianca, reflects this influence mightily. Known as “La Firenze del Sud” (The Florence of the South), Lecce showcases impressive Roman ruins, including an amphitheater in the center of town. A walking tour reveals the Roman columns and triumphal arches, perfect for history buffs who stay at Mare Mediterraneo and enjoy easy access to this ancient city.

Norman Fortifications

The Normans came next, bringing their medieval vision to Salento's landscapes. Great examples are the castles and fortresses found in Otranto and Gallipoli, their walls echoing tales of sieges and strategies. Otranto's Aragonese Castle is particularly noteworthy, offering spectacular views and steeped in history that even a short 35-minute drive makes worthwhile.

Baroque Brilliance of Lecce

The Baroque period added a layer of artistic beauty to Salento that defines much of its current identity. Lecce stands as a stunning example of Baroque architecture. Its streets are adorned with ornate churches and palaces, such as the iconic Basilica di Santa Croce and the Duomo, mere steps apart in Lecce's historical center. This Baroque extravaganza is accessible and awe-inspiring from Mare Mediterraneo, and undeniably enriches your cultural itinerary.
