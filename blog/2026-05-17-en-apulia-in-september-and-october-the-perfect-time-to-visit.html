Discovering Apulia in the Lovely Afterseason

There's something magical about Apulia, or Puglia as it's known in Italian, when summer turns to fall. The bustling beaches have quieted, the temperatures are milder, and the olive groves look like they're plucked from a postcard. While most tourists believe summer is the best time for a visit, locals know the truth: September and October are when Apulia truly shines.

Perfect Weather and Uncrowded Attractions

In Salento, the southernmost tip of Apulia, September and October bring mild and pleasant temperatures, averaging between 20-25°C (68-77°F). It's perfect for exploring the historic streets of Lecce, just a 15-minute drive from Mare Mediterraneo in Torre Chianca. This charming rental, only 400 meters from the sea, provides an idyllic home base to enjoy the region's extensive offerings.

With fewer crowds, this time of year offers a more intimate experience with the region. Visit the ancient port city of Otranto, just 35 minutes away, or the captivating beaches of Punta Prosciutto, a mere 25-minute drive. During these months, not only can you explore these stunning sites without the usual tourist rush, but you'll also find the water warm enough for a delightful swim.

A Taste of Local Culture

September and October invite you to delve deeper into Apulia's rich culture. In these months, local festivals bring a buzz to town squares with celebrations like the Olive Festival in Martano or the Grape Festival in Leverano. These events are not to be missed, providing an opportunity to savor the authentic flavors and traditional music that Salento is famous for.

Adding to the cultural tapestry is Puglia's fascinating history, recently highlighted by the story of Luigi Calabresi. A complex tale rooted in Italy's political past, it draws an intriguing contrast to the serene life in Torre Chianca, offering depth to your travels.

Easy Day Trips from Torre Chianca

One of the best aspects of basing your holiday in Torre Chianca is the ease of exploration. With Lecce only 15 minutes away, you can enjoy a leisurely afternoon admiring its Baroque architecture before heading to Gallipoli, 45 minutes away, for a sunset dinner by the sea. Porto Cesareo, just a 30-minute drive, offers marine experiences where island boat tours and coastal hikes await.
