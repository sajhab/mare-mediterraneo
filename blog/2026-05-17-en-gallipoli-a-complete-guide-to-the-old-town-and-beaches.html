Gallipoli: A Complete Guide to the Old Town and Beaches

Welcome to Gallipoli

Nestled on the Ionian coast, Gallipoli is a captivating blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Just a 45-minute drive from Torre Chianca, this charming town offers visitors an ideal escape into both its storied past and its sun-soaked beaches. Gallipoli, meaning 'beautiful city' in Greek, lives up to its name with an enchanting historical center and inviting shorelines.

Exploring the Historic Center

The heart of Gallipoli is its atmospheric Old Town, known as ''Gallipoli centro storico''. Linked to the mainland by a 17th-century bridge, it's a maze of narrow streets and alleys, each leading to unexpected baroque architecture, quaint churches, and vibrant piazzas. A must-see is the Cathedral of Sant'Agata, an exquisite example of Leccese baroque style. As you wander, don't miss the Castello Angioino, a fortress with panoramic views of the sea and city.

Top Highlights: What to See in Gallipoli

  • Gallipoli cosa vedere: Visit the Basilica Cattedrale di Sant'Agata, the symbol of the city with its stunning façade and frescoed interiors.
  • Explore the Antico Frantoio Ipogeo, an underground olive press, offering a glimpse into the town’s ancient olive oil traditions.
  • Stroll along the Riviera Nazario Sauro promenade for breathtaking sunset views.
  • Discover the charming Fontana Greca at the entrance of the historic center, one of the oldest fountains in Italy.

Gallipoli Beaches: Sun, Sand, and Sea

No visit to Gallipoli is complete without indulging in its picturesque beaches. Known for their crystal-clear waters and soft sands, these beaches are a highlight. Head to Baia Verde, a popular spot with lively beach clubs perfect for socializing or relaxing. If you prefer a more secluded experience, Punta della Suina offers a scenic landscape of dunes and Mediterranean vegetation.
