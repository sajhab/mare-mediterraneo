Cycling Through Puglia: Discover the Hidden Gems on Two Wheels

Uncover Puglia's beauty on two wheels. From Torre Chianca to Lecce, this guide reveals cycling routes and tips for an unforgettable bike tour in Salento.

Discovering Puglia on Two Wheels

If you ask me, there's no better way to explore the breathtaking landscapes of Puglia than on a bicycle. With the wind in your hair and the sun on your face, cycling in Puglia allows you to truly immerse yourself in the region's rich history and stunning natural beauty. Recently, cycling through Salento has gained popularity, thanks partially to the local buzz around Serie A season 2026/2027, with Lecce facing Venezia in their debut match. While the locals are rallying around soccer, let me rally you around our enchanting cycling routes.

Must-Try Cycling Routes in Salento

Salento offers various cycling paths for all levels of cyclists—from leisurely routes to more challenging terrains. One of my favorite routes begins at Torre Chianca, just 400 meters from the shimmering sea. From here, you can cycle straight into Lecce, a mere 15-minute ride away, where you can bask in its charming baroque architecture and indulge in an authentic Italian espresso.

For those seeking more adventure, head towards Punta Prosciutto, approximately 25 minutes away by bike. This coastal stretch is renowned for its crystal-clear waters and stunning beaches. If you have more time, consider cycling further to Porto Cesareo (30 minutes from Torre Chianca) or even Otranto, which is about 35 minutes away, offering picturesque views of medieval castles and lush olive groves.

Where to Rent Your Bicycle

Sorting out your affitto biciclette Puglia is easier than you might think. In Lecce, you’ll find several rental shops offering a range of bikes from comfy city bicycles to more robust mountain bikes. Locals usually recommend Veloservice and Ridenjoy, where the staff are friendly and offer great advice on which routes to take based on your preferences.

Staying at Mare Mediterraneo in Torre Chianca? You’re in the perfect spot to start your bicycling adventure. Our guests often appreciate the proximity to idyllic routes and welcoming locals eager to share their cycling tips.

Pro Tips for Cycling in Lecce and Beyond

  • Start Early: Puglia can get warm, especially in the peak summer months. Beginning your ride early allows you to enjoy cooler temperatures and stunning sunrises.
  • Plan Your Pit Stops: Towns like Gallipoli, 45 minutes from Torre Chianca, offer delightful local eateries and rest points to keep you refreshed. Don’t miss trying the seafood!
  • Bring a Map: Although GPS is handy, Puglia’s enchanting rural pathways can occasionally lead to signal loss. A physical map can be your savior.

There’s something magical about discovering Puglia’s hidden gems by bike—from the cultural richness of Lecce to the rugged beauty of Salento’s coastline. Whether it’s the thrill of a big match or the tranquility of a sunset ride, cycling invites you to engage with the heart of our region.
