Ostuni: Discover the Enchanting White City of Puglia

Ostuni, the dazzling white city of Puglia, awaits with its winding streets and breathtaking views. Perfect for day trips from Torre Chianca.

Photo by Tania Lyahnovich on Unsplash

The Magical Allure of Ostuni

In the heart of Puglia, where the Adriatic Sea kisses rustic landscapes, lies Ostuni, affectionately known as La Città Bianca. Its shimmering whitewashed buildings, perched on three hills, create a striking tableau against the azure skies. For travelers seeking the essence of Italian rustic beauty coupled with vibrant culture, Ostuni is a must-see.

Why is Ostuni Called the 'White City'?

Ostuni earned its nickname, the 'White City,' due to its radiant, chalky facades. Historically, residents painted their homes with lime to combat the intense summer heat and sanitize against disease. Today, this habit remains, preserving the city’s iconic luminous appearance and charm.

Exploring the Heart of Ostuni

Wander through the labyrinth of cobbled streets and discover what locals treasure. Begin your journey at the Piazza della Libertà, home to the stunningly ornate Church of San Francesco. Stroll up the mysterious, meandering pathways leading to Ostuni's cathedral, the Cathedral of Santa Maria Assunta, a 15th-century wonder with a beautiful rose window.

  • Piazza della Libertà: The bustling heart of Ostuni.
  • Cathedral of Santa Maria Assunta: Known for its incredible architecture and historical significance.
  • Chiesa di San Vito Martire: A hidden gem worth ascending the hillside streets.

Immerse Yourself in Local Culture

The city is not just about its captivating views. Every Wednesday, the local market fills the streets with vibrant stands selling everything from artisanal cheese to antique memorabilia. For a taste of the region's culinary delights, visit a traditional osteria or join a cooking class to learn how to craft authentic Puglian dishes.

How Recent Infrastructure Upgrades Enhance Your Visit

Recently, the completion of new water infrastructure works in the Villaggio Gelsi area, carefully managed by Acquedotto Pugliese, has significantly improved services for locals and visitors. This ensures that Ostuni can welcome each traveler with improved amenities, enhancing the overall experience of this timeless city.
