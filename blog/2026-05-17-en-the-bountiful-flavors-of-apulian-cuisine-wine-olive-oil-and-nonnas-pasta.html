The Bountiful Flavors of Apulian Cuisine: Wine, Olive Oil, and Nonna's Pasta

Journey through the heart of Salento and discover the exquisite Apulian cuisine. From the renowned Primitivo wine to the handmade orecchiette pasta, experience the culinary treasures that define this enchanting region.

Discovering the Culinary Heart of Salento

Perched on Italy's heel, Salento is a land where sun-kissed shores meet fertile fields, producing an extraordinary bounty that defines Apulian cuisine. A stay at Mare Mediterraneo in Torre Chianca, just 400 meters from the dazzling sea, offers an exceptional base to uncover these culinary gems. Whether you sip Primitivo wine while watching the waves or explore nearby Lecce, 15 minutes away, Salento invites you to savor its flavors.

Primitivo Wine: A Symbol of Apulia

No exploration of Apulian flavors is complete without delving into its wines. The Primitivo wine of Apulia, noted for its bold character and rich berry notes, finds its roots in the sun-drenched vineyards skirting Salento's villages. Head to Manduria, a mere 40-minute drive, to visit wineries where this robust wine is crafted with passion and tradition.

In such vineyards, tastings often include local specialties, encouraging a delightful pairing of wine with dishes like 'orecchiette al sugo,' a pasta similar to little ears, served with rich tomato sauce.

Liquid Gold: A Dive into Apulia's Olive Oil

Beyond wine, Apulia is celebrated for its olive oil—liquid gold, as the locals call it. With rolling olive groves dotting the landscape, a journey through Salento offers the chance to taste some of the finest oils. Driven by generations-old techniques, the olive oils here are celebrated for their herbal, peppery notes.

A visit to a local 'frantoio,' an olive press, offers an enlightening experience where you can see firsthand how olives are transformed into this exquisite oil. The nearby town of Carpignano Salentino, about 25 minutes from Torre Chianca, is known for such immersions in olive oil production.

The Art of Nonna's Pasta

At the heart of Apulian cuisine lies pasta, expertly crafted by Nonnas across the region. One of the region’s loves is 'orecchiette,' a small, ear-shaped pasta that perfectly holds sauces. Each village has its twist on this classic dish, often accompanied by broccoli rabe and a touch of chili flakes.

  • If you visit Lecce, seek out a culinary workshop where local Nonnas will share their revered methods of pasta-making passed down through generations.
  • An alternative is to explore the market stalls and trattorias in nearby Porto Cesareo, about 30 minutes from Torre Chianca.
