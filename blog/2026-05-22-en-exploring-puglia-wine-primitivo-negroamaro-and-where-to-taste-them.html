Exploring Puglia Wine: Primitivo, Negroamaro and Where to Taste Them

Delve into the flavors of Puglia with our insider’s guide to Primitivo and Negroamaro wines. Discover tasting spots and tips from a local perspective.

Photo by Gabriella Clare Marino on Unsplash

The Charm of Puglia Wines: An Invitation to Taste

Visitors to Puglia, especially the stunning region of Salento, are soon swept off their feet by more than just the idyllic seas and historical intrigue. Here, the deep ruby reds of Primitivo and Negroamaro wines capture the very essence of this sun-kissed Italian paradise. As you explore the region, these wines offer a unique taste of Puglia’s rich heritage, with a multitude of tasting opportunities just a short drive from Mare Mediterraneo in Torre Chianca, your perfect base.

Primitivo di Manduria: A Taste of Tradition

Just a short drive from Torre Chianca, towards the fabled coastlines of Manduria, lies a wine that is legendary among connoisseurs — Primitivo di Manduria. Known for its intense flavor and high alcohol content, Primitivo is often likened to Zinfandel but offers something distinctly Puglia. Savor these wines at Produttori di Manduria, a cooperative that has been crafting exceptional wines since 1932. Take a guided tour to understand not just the flavors, but the hard labor and love that goes into each bottle. Only 40 minutes from Mare Mediterraneo, this experience is a must for wine aficionados.

Negroamaro of Salento: The Velvet Revolution

Closer to home, the Negroamaro varietal reveals a softer, velvet-textured experience, perfect for pairing with the local cuisine that visitors delight in. Head to Leone de Castris, nestled in Salice Salentino, about a 30-minute drive from Torre Chianca. Founded in 1665, this historic winery not only offers delightful tasting sessions but also boasts a museum dedicated to the history of wine in Salento. Explore the robust, fruit-forward notes of Negroamaro, which have made it a beloved staple in Italy and beyond.

Where to Experience Local Vino: A Puglia Wine Guide

  • Enoteca Marea – Located in Lecce, just 15 minutes from your abode at Mare Mediterraneo, this enoteca showcases a curated selection of the best Puglian wines and provides expert guidance for pairing with local dishes.
  • Li Veli Winery – Situated in Cellino San Marco, this family-run operation is a testament to innovative winemaking techniques that respect tradition. The estate, a 45-minute journey from Mare Mediterraneo, offers structured tastings that pair their delectable wines with regional delicacies.
  • Castello Monaci – Perched amid vineyards and olive groves, Castello Monaci offers a historical tapestry where Negroamaro can be savored alongside southern gastronomy. 35 minutes from Torre Chianca, a tour here promises an unforgettable foray into Puglia’s winemaking history.
