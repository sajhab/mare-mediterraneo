A Weekend in Lecce: 48 Hours in the Baroque Capital

Spend a memorable weekend in Lecce, the stunning baroque capital of Salento. Discover must-see sights, local tips, and why Mare Mediterraneo is the perfect base.

Photo by Davide Perrone on Unsplash

What makes Lecce a perfect weekend destination in Puglia?

Lecce, known as the 'Florence of the South', is an architectural wonder nestled in the beautiful Salento region of Puglia. Just a 15-minute drive from Torre Chianca, where Mare Mediterraneo awaits you with open arms, the city is famous for its intricate baroque buildings and vibrant cultural scene. Over a weekend, you can explore centuries of history, savor delicious Apulian cuisine, and immerse yourself in the lively streets filled with local artisans and beautiful piazzas.

What should I do first when I arrive in Lecce?

Begin your adventure at Piazza Sant'Oronzo, the beating heart of Lecce. Here, the Roman amphitheater impressively sits alongside bustling cafés. It's the ideal spot to sip on an espresso and soak in the local atmosphere. From there, stroll to Piazza del Duomo, just a short 5-minute walk away, and marvel at the opulent Lecce Cathedral. Don’t forget to visit the Basilica di Santa Croce, a masterpiece of Baroque architecture only 3 minutes away on foot, adorned with fantastical stone carvings.

Where can I find the best local food?

For an authentic taste of Lecce, head to Osteria degli Spiriti, a beloved spot where locals gather to enjoy traditional pasta dishes like orecchiette with turnip tops. If you’re craving something sweet, try a pasticciotto at Caffè Alvino — it’s a mouth-watering pastry you cannot miss. These culinary delights are scattered throughout the city's winding streets, offering the perfect opportunity to eat like a local.

What are some must-see sights around Lecce?

If time allows, plan a short excursion to nearby treasures like Otranto, just 35 minutes away, where you can visit the majestic Otranto Cathedral. Or venture 45 minutes to Gallipoli with its picturesque old town perched on a limestone island. Don’t miss the stunning beaches at Punta Prosciutto and Porto Cesareo, located 25 and 30 minutes from Torre Chianca, respectively. The diverse beauty of Salento is at your fingertips if you choose Mare Mediterraneo as your base.

Where can I wind down after a day of exploring?

As you sip and savor some tagliere (meat and cheese platter), reflect on the day's adventures and plan tomorrow’s. These moments beautifully capture the laid-back elegance of Salento living.
